This directory has inputs for a full CI calculation for the NH3 molecule using the cc-pvdz basis set.  There are 29 orbitals and 10 electrons.  Python code was used to generate all combinations of bit-strings with 29 spatial orbitals occupied by 5 alpha electrons. There are 118755 distinct alpha bit-strings.  The dimension of the full CI space, binomial(29,5) squared = 1.41e10.  This makes a good large-system test.  A full CI calculation was attempted using PySCF, but that failed with 32-bit integer overflow, due to the large subspace dimension. PySCF yielded Hartree-Fock energy -56.19535340 H, and CCSD(T) energy = -56.40325954 H.  The SBD full CI result is -56.40385561 H, just slightly lower than the CCSD(T) value.
